02 / 11 / Problem Statement
The Gap
SPORTS IS A LANGUAGE.
And not everyone grew up speaking it. Sports culture is woven into everyday social life at school, at work, and at family gatherings. For millions of people, walking into a conversation about last night's game feels like landing somewhere without knowing the local language.
The problem is not a lack of interest. It is a lack of access. There has never been a low-barrier, time-respectful entry point into sports knowledge built for the majority of Americans who are on the outside looking in. Sports Talk is that entry point.

03 / 11 / Origin Story
Where This Came From
THE PROBLEM, LIVED FIRSTHAND.
This app came from a real experience. Growing up without a background in sports meant that whenever the conversation turned to last night's game, there was a choice: fake it, go quiet, or fall further behind.
Even when one sport clicked, there was always another. The time investment felt endless. Eventually the love for sports came on its own terms, but the path was much longer and harder than it needed to be.
"I never knew what to say when conversations switched to sports when I was in middle school. I just wanted to have something to add. I spent years teaching myself about sports and eventually found that I loved it, but I wish I had a helping hand along the way."

THE OUTSIDER
THE LAPSED FAN
THE ANALYST
Does not follow sports. Little to no baseline knowledge. But lives in environments where sports conversations happen constantly: work, school, relationships, rivalry week, the Olympics. The exclusion is real and recurring. They want in, but the barrier feels impossible to clear.
Used to follow sports. Life got busy: career, family, other priorities. Cannot watch games anymore, but still cares. Wants to show up to the watch party ready, not lost. Wants to text college friends about the game like they used to.
Watches every game, follows every trade, argues every MVP pick. Wants more than scores: advanced metrics, contrarian takes, argument fuel. Crucially, a basketball diehard trying to learn F1 or cricket still needs the novice experience for that new sport.
Core Pain
Every conversation feels like a test they did not study for. Every new sport is a new language. Every major event is another reminder they are on the outside.
Core Pain
Conversations have context they no longer have. They do not want a full education, just enough to stay connected to the people they love through sports.
Core Need
Deeper knowledge across more sports. A way to onboard into unfamiliar territory without starting from zero. The level is per-sport, not per-person.
05 / 11 / User Research
Validated Pain Points
THE DATA BACKS IT UP.
The personal experience behind Sports Talk is not unique. Research across public platforms and market data confirms this is a widespread, underserved problem with no existing product solution built for it.
The opportunity
Sports betting has colonized fan product design. Every major app now integrates odds. A genuinely educational, honest alternative is a real market gap and a position people actively want.
FINDING 01
62% of Americans say they follow sports "not too" or "not at all" closely. Yet sports conversations are unavoidable in most workplaces, campuses, and social settings. The gap between expectation and knowledge is enormous.

06 / 11 / Core MVP Features
What the product does
THE FEATURE SET
01
⚡
GAME DAY BRIEF
A 90-second pre-event read: 5 numbered talking points, no more. Push notification fires before big games. Walk into any watch party ready to hold a real conversation. This is the core retention loop.
Flagship
02
📊
LEVEL-AWARE FEED
The same story told three ways. Novice, Casual, Diehard. Same information, different vocabulary and depth. Switch levels per sport, not per account. A basketball diehard is still a novice for cricket.
Core
03
💬
TAPPABLE TERMS
Bold underlined terms in every brief. Tap once, get a plain-English definition right there in the text. No separate glossary. Sports vocabulary explained exactly where it appears.
Core
04
📚
NARRATIVE LAYER
Sports is about storylines, not just scores. Each brief surfaces the underlying narrative: the MVP race, the surprise team, the record chase. Context that makes news meaningful to an outsider.
Insight
05
🏈
CRASH COURSES
For sports needing baseline context (hockey, F1, college football, cricket, the Olympics), a primer card appears first. Rules, key terms, who matters. Before any news, before any opinion.
Education
06
🔔
SMART NOTIFICATIONS
"Super Bowl is in 3 days. Read your brief." Event-aware alerts surface the Game Day Brief at exactly the right moment: before you need it, not after. Includes the Olympics, rivalry weeks, and finals.
Retention
07 / 11 / How It Works
User Flow
THE EXPERIENCE
01
ONBOARD
Pick your level, select sports you care about or want to learn, and state your goal: social confidence, staying current, or advanced takes.
02
GET FEED
AI generates a brief per sport at your level: plain-English summary, talking points, tappable terms, key narrative, and a visual stat when relevant.
03
LEARN
Crash courses for unfamiliar sports. Inline term definitions. Narrative context. The app meets users where they are and grows with them over time.
04
GAME DAY
Notification fires before the big event. Read 5 points in 90 seconds. Walk in ready. The loop: brief, event, return.
Graduation path
Users start at whichever level fits. As comfort grows, they move up, or drop to novice for a new sport. A basketball diehard exploring F1 is still a novice for F1. Sports Talk works for the Olympics, a Super Bowl party, a work happy hour, or a first date with a sports fan.

09 / 11 / Key Assumptions
What we are testing
WHAT WE ASSUME.
Every MVP is built on assumptions. These are the beliefs Sports Talk tests before scaling to a full product.
What success looks like
A user reads the Game Day Brief, goes to a watch party, holds a real conversation, and opens the app before the next event. That loop is the entire product thesis: brief, event, return.
💬
Social Driver
Primary motivation to use Sports Talk is social confidence, not intrinsic curiosity. Users open it because they are about to be around sports fans.
⏱
Time Willingness
Users will invest 90 seconds on a pre-event brief. Content must be optimized for that window: 5 points, plain language, immediately usable in conversation.
📲
Push Notification Acceptance
Users will allow notifications for event-triggered briefs because the notification is timed and genuinely useful, not ambient noise.
🎓
Level Fluidity
Users will set themselves as novice for unfamiliar sports without ego friction. The per-sport level system removes the stigma from not knowing something.
🚫
Anti-Betting Positioning
A meaningful audience actively wants a sports knowledge tool that is not a betting platform. The anti-gambling position is itself a feature, not just a stance.
